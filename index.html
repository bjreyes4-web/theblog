WHY PARIS? WHY THRIFTING?

This Spring break I had a trip planned to Paris! It was only my second time ever visiting. The first time I focused on souvenirs for others that I barely got anything for myself. So, this time, it’s all about me and what I enjoy: which is thrifting!

I have heard amazing things about thrifting and vintage in Paris - I did want to focus on the lower price range of thrifting so I limited myself to more than 25€ per shop. I was looking for more unique pieces and brands that were either hard to find or not available in the US.

The three shops I chose were pretty known for their lower prices so I was super intrigued and wanted to see if that was accurate or not. I wanted to focus on the ambiance itself, whether or not they had fitting rooms, how big of a selection they had, what brands were seen often, and if I would go back or not.

Safe to say, my carry-on was very full by after visiting the shops...

KILO SHOP

This was my first stop on my trip, I had seen this location online due to it being a shop that charges you by the weight, rather than by item. I was super intrigued and saw they had multiple locations.

This specific location that I chose was huge! Two floors and each floor had racks and racks of clothing. A plus was that they had weigh stations scattered within the store so you could weigh as you shop. I personally did not use the weighing stations since I couldn’t for the life of me understand the weight to euro ratio…

I think that was the only downside — some items were labeled as €20, €30, €40, and €60 per kilo.

While searching through racks, I could not find anything that would fit my personal style, but they had a bunch of trendy pieces and sections! A whole lot of denim, vintage fur jackets, leather jackets, jerseys, statement belts, and even a section for polka dotted tops.

The store itself was incredibly clean. They also had fitting rooms!

My total came out to €20 for two purses. Not too shabby, but definitely pricier than expected. I would go back to window-shop, but not any large purchases.

I would say this store would be a 7/10 or 3.5 stars for me. Very unique finds while also having sections for trendy pieces from the EU & US!

GUERRISOL

This is the second shop I visited. When I was researching budget-friendly locations, this shop came up plenty of times. The best way to describe it was that it’s Paris’ equivalent to Goodwill in the US.

The location I visited was definitely more in a residential area and not the famous thrifting streets. I chose this location specifically to avoid large crowds and find more authentic pieces. There were brands such as Uniqlo, Zara, and funnily enough — Monoprix (which is their version of a Target).

I purchased one graphic tee, one sweater top, and a cheetah print belt and in total it was 15€! Still under my budget.

They also had three open fitting rooms that were available which was a plus! I would say this is a 9/10.

The ambiance was a bit underwhelming, but unsurprising based on reviews and how it resembles a Goodwill. Despite that, I really liked this place! It reminded me of thrifting in SF. I would go back!

I would say this is a 9/10 or 4.5 stars! Everything was reasonably priced and I did not see many signs that went over 20€.

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

FIVE VINTAGE

This was my third & last stop! Man was this place huge… It was essentially a warehouse full of a bajillion bins overflowing with clothing pieces from denim, leather coats, belts, bags, and so much more.

This area felt very secluded and residential and I went earlier in the morning so there was barely anyone else. It seemed as though there was one worker for the whole warehouse and he did everything from security, clean-up, and he was the cashier as well.

I won’t lie, this place was definitely overwhelming. Since it was a humongous space with so much within every area you turned to, you honestly would need probably most of the day to look through racks.

Since it was a humongous space with so much within every area you turned to, you honestly would need probably most of the day to look through racks of items you are looking for. Similarly to the Kilo Shop, everything you purchase was based on the weight.

The downside to that was that I was again super confused as to the weight, being from the US and not using kg however it ranged from 5€ to 10€ so it was much more affordable than the Kilo Shop in my opinion.

I purchased two decently heavy pairs of pants, one denim and one a thick corduroy texture. My total was 20€ and it was close to my budget, but they were both unique pieces I had never seen anywhere else.

They also had racks based on clothing item: shirts, sweaters, denim, leather/fur coats, belts, and so much more. They had some amazing brands such as Levi’s, Wrangler, Uniqlo, etc.

The biggest downside is that there are no fitting rooms. Another thing was that certain clothing items were super frayed and had big holes in them, but it was only a few.

I would say that if you have a lot of time, a lot of luggage space, and are an experienced thrifter, this would be a very good place to check out! Would definitely go back.

I would rate this place a 8/10 or 4/5 stars. I thought it was a great option for unique finds, but no fitting room and some frayed finds took a few points off.
